The writing loop

Built around the moment
a line becomes a verse.

No feeds, no publishing pressure, no maze of setup. Just an instrument that listens closely while you work.

Write without changing how you write.

The editor stays clean and direct. Rhyme color appears under your words without moving the caret or breaking your rhythm.

Teach it the way you hear.

Merge families, reject a match, or teach a pronunciation. Cadence adapts to your ear instead of forcing one definition of rhyme.

Step back and see the shape.

Flow Lanes, syllable view, and density reveal the structure of a draft. Use the signal, then return to the page.

More than an editor

Every loose bar has somewhere to go.

Catch fragments in Notes. Connect scraps to songs with backlinks. Move ideas across the Verse Board until they are ready. Export whenever the work needs to leave Cadence.

Local by design

Your vault is a folder.
Your songs are files.

On desktop, Cadence stores songs and notes as plain Markdown. Edit them with another tool, back them up yourself, or place the vault under version control. The rhyme engine works offline, too.
